About "Land of Grace"

Discovering the heart and soul of Venezuela

Why "Land of Grace"?

In 1498, during his third voyage to the Americas, Christopher Columbus arrived at the eastern coast of what is now Venezuela. Overwhelmed by the lush vegetation, the mild climate, the abundant fresh water, and the warmth of the indigenous people, he named this place "Tierra de Gracia" (Land of Grace).

This project honors that legacy by showcasing the extraordinary natural beauty, cultural richness, and unforgettable experiences that Venezuela continues to offer to travelers from around the world.

The Mission of This Project

🏕️

Promote activities

Encourage travelers to explore diverse outdoor adventures, from hiking ancient tepuis to enjoying Caribbean water sports.

📸

Showcase Beauty

Highlight the unmatched ecological diversity and breathtaking landscapes that make Venezuela a unique destination.

🤝

Promote Respect

Foster a deep appreciation and respect for the local culture, indigenous communities, and our natural heritage.

Scope

This website was conceived and developed by Félix Barreto as the final project for the Web Development course (WDD 131) at BYU-Pathway Worldwide.

While it serves as an academic milestone, its true vision extends beyond the classroom: it is designed to be the foundational starting point for a broader initiative dedicated to promoting the natural wonders, culture, and tourism potential of Venezuela to the world.
